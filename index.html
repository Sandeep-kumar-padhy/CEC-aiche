CHEM-E-CAR

Chem-E-Car is a hitherto small-scale vehicle that is wholly powered by chemical reactions. There is an annual Chem-E-Car competition organized by AIChE for students from different chapters to compete to compete their respective cars againts one another in a controlled and manipulated environment. Chem-E-Car basically needs a starting or powering reaction, circuitry for control and finally a stopping reaction. We can also add some more controls and a little intelligence to our car by adding a microprocessor like Arduino. More of these shall be covered in the sections.

“AIChE's annual Chem-E-Car Competition® engages college students in designing and constructing a car powered by a chemical energy source, that will safely carry a specified load over a given distance and stop.”

SUB-TEAMS:

Starting Reaction:

This team takes care about the starting reaction to be used in the Chem-E-Car. Starting reaction, as the name suggests, starts the car but it is responsible for powering the car until it finally stops. So, it is basically a chemical cell. So far, we have tried out various chemical reactions and cells to power the vehicle. Last year, we tried out two different cells in two of our different cars, namely thermo peltier cell and Al air cell. The team has to test out the proposed cell in multiple conditions and proportions of chemical used so that it truly stands out in all conditions.

Electronics:

The circuit team plays a vital role as it sets up the control of the car, starting from the motors attached with the wheels to LDR that makes the motor stop when the stopping reaction finally happens. This is not all. They are also encased with making the circuits as uncomplicated and less power-consuming as possible. It also designs and embeds the sensor system which makes the car function in the required way in the specified time.

Design:

The mechanics team is entitled with the responsibility of making the car robust and design the whole mechanical structure, They decide the position of all the elements of the car starting from the chassis to the wheels and also the quality and size of the elements. The plan group must consolidate and organize the reactor, control source, microcontrollers and wiring into the car, so that it behaves in the desired way.

Stopping Reaction:

Stopping reaction marks the end of the car’s journey. This is facilitated by the stopping reaction team which decides upon a proper reaction which can incorporate a final stopping of the car in the smoothest way possible. The team prototypes the reaction for various proportions and manipulates the time of the reaction so that it matches the requirements of the competition. The required time, load and distance are provided right before the competition. So, the team has to be prepared for all the different requirements.
